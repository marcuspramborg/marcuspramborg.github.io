Study Strategies for ADHD

  • Break it down: Divide large tasks into 5-10 minute chunks
  • Use timers: Work in short sprints with regular breaks
  • Multi-sensory learning: Read aloud, use colors, draw diagrams
  • Active recall: Test yourself instead of just re-reading
  • Change locations: Study in different places to boost memory

Focus Enhancement

  • Remove distractions: Put phone away, use website blockers
  • Background noise: Try white noise, brown noise, or instrumental music
  • Fidget tools: Stress balls, fidget cubes for hands
  • Standing desk: Alternate between sitting and standing
  • Two-task rule: If restless, combine studying with light movement

Anxiety Management

  • Progressive relaxation: Tense and release muscle groups
  • Grounding technique: Name 5 things you see, 4 you hear, 3 you feel
  • Positive self-talk: "I am prepared, I can do this"
  • Breathing exercises: Use the 4-7-8 technique above
  • Perspective: This is one test, not your entire future

Effective Break Ideas

  • Movement breaks: Stretch, walk, do jumping jacks
  • Hydration: Drink water, have a healthy snack
  • Fresh air: Step outside or open a window
  • Quick meditation: 2-3 minutes of deep breathing
  • Reward yourself: Small treats for completing sections
